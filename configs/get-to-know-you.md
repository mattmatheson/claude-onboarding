# Get to Know You — Claude Onboarding Interview

> **Instructions for Claude:** Read this file, then interview the user conversationally. Don't dump all questions at once — ask 2-3 at a time, respond to their answers naturally, then continue. After the interview, compile the answers into a personalized CLAUDE.md file.

## Part 1: About You

Get a picture of who this person is and what they're working on.

- What's your name and what do you do? (job, side projects, hobbies)
- What are you trying to build right now? What's the dream project?
- How would you describe your technical experience? (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- What programming languages/frameworks have you used before?
- What's your development environment? (Mac, Windows, Linux? What editor?)
- Do you have multiple computers you work across?

## Part 2: About Your Work

Understand their goals and daily workflow.

- What kind of things do you want to build with Claude? (dashboards, websites, automation, data analysis, creative projects)
- Do you have clients or is this personal projects?
- What tools do you already use daily? (Notion, Figma, Slack, specific apps)
- How do you currently keep track of notes, tasks, and project info?
- Do you use version control (git)? How comfortable are you with it?
- Have you deployed anything to production? (Vercel, Netlify, a VPS, etc.)

## Part 3: How You Learn and Work

Calibrate communication style and teaching approach.

- Do you prefer step-by-step explanations or "just do it and I'll ask if confused"?
- When something breaks, do you want to understand why or just want it fixed?
- How do you feel about the terminal/command line? Comfortable? Intimidated?
- Do you prefer visual dashboards or text-based workflows?
- What's your attention span like for technical setup? (Can do 2 hours straight vs. prefers 15-minute chunks)
- Anything that annoys you about working with AI? Things you want me to always/never do?

---

## After the Interview

Compile the answers into a CLAUDE.md file at `~/.claude/CLAUDE.md` with sections for:
1. About Me (name, role, experience level)
2. Current Projects (what they're building)
3. Code Preferences (stack, style, conventions)
4. Tools & Environment (what they use)
5. Communication Style (how they want Claude to behave)
6. Safety Rules (standard security practices)

Ask the user to review and approve the CLAUDE.md before saving it.
